The site selected from Awwwards

1. Why I chose this site

I chose the Bleu de Chanel website because it represents the intersection of luxury branding and digital storytelling. As a product site for one of the world’s most recognizable fashion houses, it balances prestige, simplicity, and emotional engagement through carefully crafted visuals and sound design.

2. First impression

The first impression is one of refinement and calm power. The immersive hero section, subtle transitions, and dark-blue palette convey masculinity and sophistication. The atmosphere feels cinematic, reinforcing the identity of the fragrance itself.

3. Structure and UX

The navigation follows a clear hierarchical structure. The product variations (Eau de Toilette, Eau de Parfum, Le Parfum) are organized within distinct sections, each accompanied by imagery and descriptive storytelling. Scrolling through the page feels fluid thanks to large content blocks and whitespace that reduce visual fatigue. The menu is consistent with the Chanel ecosystem, providing intuitive access to other product categories.

4. Visual design and branding

The design embraces minimalism: strong use of white and deep blue tones, combined with elegant typography and balanced compositions. The photography is of premium quality and communicates the luxurious essence of the brand. Every element reinforces Chanel’s heritage — timeless, modern, and understated.

5. Interactions and animations

Transitions between sections are smooth and elegant. Micro-interactions on buttons and links provide gentle feedback without being distracting. The parallax movement of the fragrance bottles enhances the storytelling, while maintaining a restrained rhythm that matches the calm tone of the visuals.

6. Usability and performance

The site performs well on both desktop and mobile. Loading times are optimized considering the high-resolution media. The text contrast meets accessibility standards, and navigation is keyboard-friendly. However, the reliance on heavy video backgrounds could slow down performance on slower networks.

7. Critical reflection

The experience clearly prioritizes emotion and brand storytelling over functional exploration. While this fits a luxury context, it could be less accessible to users seeking quick product comparison or purchase information. Nonetheless, the balance between aesthetics and usability is admirable.

8. Conclusion

Bleu de Chanel’s website exemplifies digital luxury — restrained yet powerful. It teaches how subtle motion, color discipline, and structured storytelling can create an immersive, elegant user experience.

AB Partners – Webby Awards Selection

1. Why I chose this site

AB Partners’ website is an excellent example of a creative agency’s online identity. It was recognized at the Webby Awards for its innovative layout, showcasing how design agencies use their own sites as a portfolio of motion, interaction, and design thinking.

2. First impression

The first impression is dynamic, bold, and confident. The site immediately presents strong motion design and a playful use of typography, signaling creativity and modernity. The use of large type and asymmetric grids creates an energetic rhythm.

3. Structure and UX

The navigation is minimalist but clear. A compact top menu leads to key sections such as Work, Studio, and Contact. The site uses a single-page structure with smooth scrolling transitions. Each project case study opens seamlessly, avoiding disruptive page reloads and keeping the user in a continuous flow.

4. Visual design and typography

The color palette combines black, white, and beige tones with bold accent colors depending on the project section. The typography is expressive and changes dynamically across pages — sometimes geometric, sometimes condensed — giving personality and rhythm to the design. The visual hierarchy is experimental but still readable.

5. Interactions and animations

Motion is central to the AB Partners experience. Subtle scroll effects, smooth transitions, and animated grid layouts create a sense of movement that reflects the agency’s expertise in digital production. Micro-details, like hover effects and smooth easing, make the interface feel responsive and alive.

6. Usability and performance

Despite its heavy use of motion graphics, the website loads quickly and runs smoothly on modern browsers. Mobile responsiveness is strong — the layout adapts neatly without losing personality. Accessibility could be improved by increasing text contrast in some sections and ensuring focus states for interactive elements.

7. Critical reflection

AB Partners prioritizes creativity and brand expression over conventional structure. This is appropriate for an agency site, but some users might find navigation less predictable. The design challenges standard web conventions intentionally — turning the site itself into a showcase of their creative philosophy.

8. Conclusion

AB Partners demonstrates how design and motion can serve as a brand language. The website successfully balances artistry and usability, providing an inspiring example of interactive storytelling in the creative industry.
